A two-sided marketplace. Employers post jobs (some free, some paid), candidates browse and filter. Two distinct user shapes meeting on the same surface, which is the whole interesting problem.

Auth went to Clerk on purpose. Hand-rolling email verification, password reset, OAuth, and session management for a side project is a way to ship nothing for three weeks; Clerk solves it in an afternoon and gets out of the way. The cost is a vendor in the auth path, which I judged acceptable for a project of this scope. Data sits in Postgres behind Prisma, with the schema modeling the asymmetry of the two sides: an employer record owns many job postings, a candidate record owns applications and saved listings. Prisma's relations and type generation made the read paths pleasant and kept the API routes honest about what they were returning.

Company logos and job-related assets live in Vercel Blob storage. Putting binaries in Postgres is a known mistake, and Vercel Blob is the lowest-friction option when the rest of the stack is already on Vercel. Shadcn for components, Tailwind for layout, Zod at the form boundary.

If I were extending this, the next move is search: full-text indexing on titles and descriptions, with proper filters on location, role, and salary band. A job board is a search product wearing a feed.
